v2.12.104

- Added: Pause auto-sync — a single switch in Settings to stop background syncs without losing your intervals.
- Added: Sync on window focus — quietly refresh when you return to the app (at most once every 5 minutes).
- Added: Power BI refresh interval — Power BI revenue and insights now refresh on their own schedule, separate from the main sync.
- Added: Sync scope toggles — pick whether each sync touches Dynamics 365, Power BI, or your pending changes.
- Added: Last-run line under each interval, so you can tell at a glance whether the background sync is actually firing.
- Added: Sync history retention — old entries are cleaned up automatically; default keeps the last 90 days.
- Added: Show sync toasts can now be toggled from the Sync tab too.
- Improved: Quick preset buttons next to every interval (15/30/60 etc.) and clearer feedback when you type a value outside the allowed range — it now clamps and tells you instead of silently ignoring the input.
- Added: a "mute non-critical toasts" master switch in Notifications — flip it on during demos or screen-sharing and only real errors come through.
- Added: toast duration setting so you can make alerts stickier or faster to dismiss.
- Added: native Windows notifications — alerts can now appear in the system notification centre even when the app is minimized.
- Added: optional beep on warnings and errors.
- Added: quiet hours — pick a start and end time and the app stops popping non-critical toasts during that window.
- Added: a "daily digest" option that bundles all launch reminders (overdue, due today, upcoming, stale, expiring) into a single toast instead of firing them one after another.
- Added: an "opportunity expiring soon" reminder that flags opportunities whose expiration date is coming up within the number of days you choose.
- Added: sync toast verbosity — pick between "Success + errors", "Errors only", or "Silent".
- Added: sync failure threshold — for example, only show a failure toast after three consecutive failed sync attempts, so one flaky network blip doesn't bother you.
